# Jennifer Gaudette: Complete professional profile for WorkSapien website build

**Jennifer (Jenn) Gaudette is a Toronto-area career and health coach who founded WorkSapien to help mid-career professionals transform their relationship with work.** She holds the ICF's highest credential — Master Certified Coach (MCC) — placing her among the top 3% of coaches worldwide, alongside a Master of Environmental Studies (MES) and Career Development Practitioner (CDP) designation. With **20+ years of combined experience** spanning career transition, wellness, human resources, and environmental behaviour change, she operates at the uncommon intersection of career strategy and holistic wellbeing. Her brand promise is encapsulated in WorkSapien's tagline: **"Humanity at work."**

---

## Career trajectory from psychology grad to master coach

Jennifer's professional story begins with an undergraduate **degree in Psychology**, after which she moved to Australia — intending to stay a few months but remaining for roughly **10 years**. Her first post-graduation job was in a retail shoe store, not the pioneering psychology role she'd envisioned. This experience of navigating career misalignment across countries became foundational to her later coaching philosophy.

Upon returning to Toronto, she built a career across multiple domains over two decades:

- **Career Transition Consultant at Gallagher (Arthur J. Gallagher & Co.)** — a major global brokerage and HR consulting firm with over 400 HR and Benefits professionals across Canada. Gallagher's career transition division provides outplacement, coaching, and workforce transformation consulting. Jennifer's role involved guiding individuals through professional transitions, giving her extensive corporate-side experience.

- **Founder of Upspring Wellness** — originally launched as a workplace wellness consultancy in the HR space, this venture evolved when Jennifer recognized her passion for helping people find purpose-aligned work. As she describes it: *"My first client was me. This work has evolved into a health-meets-life-meets-career coaching practice where I help people to stay in action on their own reinvention and not settle for less than their full potential."* Upspring offered 1-on-1 and group coaching programs, helping parents, entrepreneurs, career transitioners, and busy professionals. The website (upspringwellness.ca) currently shows a "Coming Soon" page, suggesting the brand has been largely absorbed into WorkSapien.

- **Founder of WorkSapien** — her current primary venture and brand. WorkSapien is described as *"an integrated career and health coaching services company with a mission to elevate the wellbeing for millions of humans at work."*

- **Facilitator at Canada Coach Academy (CCA)** — she has served as a facilitator at this coaching education institution, which is led by Nathalie Blais, an ICF Master Certified Coach.

- **Career Coach in Residence at Toronto Public Library (2020–2021)** — Jennifer was one of four inaugural coaches selected for this community program, which ran from September 28–December 4, 2020 and February 1–April 9, 2021. Supported by the **RBC Foundation** and the **Friends of the Toronto Public Library, South Chapter**, she provided remote career and job search help to younger adults ages 18–29, covering résumé writing, interview preparation, job searching, and LinkedIn profile development.

## WorkSapien coaching services and methodology

WorkSapien positions itself at the distinctive intersection of career coaching and health coaching — a niche Jennifer describes as **"finding and creating work, and ways of working, that have health benefits for individuals and organizations."**

**Mission statement:** *"Elevate the wellbeing for millions of humans at work."*

**Target audience:** Mid-career professionals, leaders, and executives in professional services and knowledge work. On her Alignable profile, she further specifies her ideal client as *"a woman in career transition or at a crossroads who is ready to get clarity, get healthy and get started reinventing herself."* Her Noomii profile focuses on *"mid-career professionals who are experiencing career-related health challenges."*

**The four pain points WorkSapien addresses** (taken verbatim from the website):
1. *"You've tried the wait-and-see-if-things-change approach and work life has not improved, the feeling of stuck-ness is transforming into an existential crisis"*
2. *"You (and your team) are in a cycle of overwork, burnout and recovery and you want to create healthier work habits or consider bigger changes"*
3. *"You are experiencing high stress levels directly related to work pressures and are ready to stop stressing without sacrificing learning and growth"*
4. *"You have fallen out of love with the work that you do and are prepared to reflect on and re-negotiate, pivot or transition to healthier opportunity"*

**The Worksapien System** — Jennifer has developed a proprietary framework visualized as a three-circle Venn diagram on her homepage. While the specific labels are embedded in the graphic (not text-extractable), the system appears to integrate career strategy, health/wellness, and personal identity or values alignment.

**Program structure:** Her flagship offering is a **12-week / 90-day structured coaching program** featuring weekly discussions and exercises. Multiple client testimonials reference this specific format. The program incorporates industry-standard skill assessment tools that she then personalizes and extends. Core elements include values identification, strengths discovery, career visioning, and actionable goal-setting.

**Positive Intelligence (PQ) Program:** WorkSapien has offered (or previously offered) the **PQ mental fitness program** — an app-based course and mindfulness program designed to *"understand and address self-sabotage, create a daily mindfulness habit and build confidence in moving forward on big goals."* Product pages for this program currently return 404 errors, suggesting possible restructuring.

**Booking:** Free 30-minute introductory calls are offered via Calendly (calendly.com/worksapien/30min). A "Coach Strategy Session" was also previously listed as a first meeting to test-drive a session and determine alignment.

**Promised outcomes for clients:**
- Move forward more sustainably with expanded capacity to be a changemaker
- Feel a sense of control by living and working with more intention
- Experience more freedom in when, where, and how they work
- Take better care of themselves without sacrificing career growth
- Realign with what is most important and figure out "who you are now and what you want next"
- Initiate a bold new career chapter designed by and for the client

## Personal brand positioning and messaging architecture

Jennifer's brand operates on a clear and differentiated positioning: she is not just a career coach, and not just a health coach, but a coach who works at the **intersection of both**. This is a distinct niche in the coaching market.

**Brand identity elements:**
- **Primary tagline:** "Humanity at work"
- **Secondary theme:** "Human Sustainability"
- **Instagram tagline:** "Career and Health Coaching Programs to transform work from being a source of depletion into a source of well-being"
- **Company description:** "An integrated career and health coaching services company"

**Core brand narrative:** Jennifer's personal story — of leaving a psychology degree behind to work in a shoe store in Australia, eventually building a career in HR and wellness consulting, then reinventing herself as a coach — mirrors the transformation she promises clients. Her origin story for Upspring Wellness is particularly revealing: she was originally running a workplace wellness consultancy when she realized her own values had shifted, and she became her own first client. This narrative of personal reinvention is central to her credibility.

**Key language patterns and themes:**
- "Wellbeing-first approach" to professional development
- "Redefine your relationship with work"
- Work as either "a source of depletion" or "a source of well-being"
- Career "reinvention" (not just transition)
- "Career cobwebs" that accumulate from being in the workforce
- Finding alignment between "who you are, your values, your strengths" and your work
- The concept that work is *"a powerful influence on every area of wellbeing, often a root cause of health issues or wellness benefits, with the potential to elevate or deteriorate what is valued most in life"*

**Tone and voice:** Warm, empathetic, direct, and purpose-driven. She uses conversational yet professional language. Her messaging avoids corporate jargon in favor of human-centered language about purpose, values, and wellbeing.
